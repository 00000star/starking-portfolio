Case study

Disaster Mesh Communications

Disaster Mesh Communications is an offline-first coordination PWA for neighborhoods, shelters, and small response groups. It assumes internet service may fail and that coordination still has to continue through local storage, export/import, radio plans, and printable handoff.

Problem

Small groups under disaster conditions often coordinate with a mix of paper, chat apps, and memory. Those systems break down quickly when connectivity is poor, information becomes stale, or devices need to hand off work to other people.

Approach

  • Store the workspace locally in the browser so the app remains useful when connectivity drops.
  • Design the data around actual coordination units: people, supplies, tasks, radio, and notes.
  • Support JSON export/import to move the incident state between devices.
  • Keep a print handoff mode for non-digital fallback.

What it demonstrates

  • Local-first architecture with IndexedDB as product strategy, not just implementation detail.
  • Operational UX design for chaotic conditions and low-trust data.
  • Attention to stale information, handoff, privacy limits, and safety notes.
  • How to scope a public-good MVP around the smallest useful coordination loop.

Current shipped features

  • Incident dashboard and safety notes.
  • People/household registry and supply requests/offers.
  • Task board, radio plan, and map notes.
  • JSON handoff plus print mode.

Tradeoffs

  • The app cannot guarantee rescue, logistics, or data accuracy.
  • Privacy must stay minimal in community incident contexts.
  • Coordination value depends on disciplined operators and current information.

Outcome

The project is a strong portfolio proof piece because it combines product judgement, offline-first systems thinking, and realistic emergency constraints. It shows the ability to design for degraded conditions instead of assuming perfect networks and ideal users.
