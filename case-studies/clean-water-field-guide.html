Case study

Clean Water Field Guide

Clean Water Field Guide is an offline-first emergency water safety MVP for households, schools, and field teams. The project is deliberately conservative: it helps users reason about water risk while staying explicit about what the tool cannot guarantee.

Open repository Read spec
StatusMVP repo with validation and offline surface
StackStatic web app, localStorage, manifest, service worker, print mode
ScopeRisk questionnaire, treatment guidance, bleach helper, storage checklist
User valueSafer water decision support during outages and emergencies

Problem

In emergencies, people need fast water guidance but face dangerous uncertainty. Generic “survival tips” often overstate confidence or fail to separate biological contamination from chemical risk.

Approach

  • Use a short risk questionnaire to reduce the decision space.
  • Keep guidance conservative and citation-visible.
  • Include print mode and offline behavior for low-connectivity contexts.
  • Persist the latest assessment locally with localStorage.

What makes it a strong case study

  • It shows restraint: the product explicitly says what it does not do.
  • It uses official source material as a design dependency, not decorative footnotes.
  • It demonstrates how safety-sensitive UX should prioritize trust boundaries over false certainty.
  • It shows that a small static PWA can still embody careful product judgement.

Current shipped features

  • Questionnaire for source, clarity, odor, contamination context, and user vulnerability.
  • Boiling guidance and bleach dose helper.
  • Safe-storage checklist and printable emergency sheet.
  • Visible links to CDC, EPA, and WHO materials.

Tradeoffs

  • The app must stay conservative even when users want certainty.
  • Public-health trust requires explicit limitation language.
  • Expansion needs careful review to avoid harmful overreach.

Outcome

This project demonstrates a type of engineering signal that many portfolios avoid: safety-aware product boundaries. It proves the ability to combine offline usability, print accessibility, and source-backed guidance in a domain where careless UX can cause harm.
