# Plain-Language Rights Explainer

## Mission

Build careful, jurisdiction-aware explainers that turn complex rules into plain-language checklists for tenants, workers, patients, immigrants, and consumers.

## Problem

Rights information is often written for lawyers, hidden in PDFs, or spread across government pages. People need clear next steps, evidence lists, deadlines, and referral paths without being misled into thinking the tool is legal advice.

## MVP

- Guided question flow.
- Plain-language summary.
- Evidence checklist.
- Deadline reminders.
- Referral/resource directory.
- Source citations and last-reviewed dates.

## Technical Plan

- Static PWA.
- Rules stored as structured JSON.
- Jurisdiction selector.
- Print-friendly action plan.
- Source metadata validation.

## Safety Rules

- Do not claim to provide legal advice.
- Show jurisdiction and last-reviewed date.
- Cite official or reputable sources.
- Encourage professional help for high-stakes situations.
- Avoid guaranteeing eligibility or outcomes.

## Portfolio Value

This project demonstrates responsible legal-adjacent UX, structured rules, source discipline, accessibility, and careful limitation handling.
