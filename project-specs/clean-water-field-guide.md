# Clean Water Field Guide

## Mission

Build a careful offline guide for identifying risky water, choosing treatment methods, storing treated water, and understanding when basic treatment is not enough.

## Problem

People often hear simplistic advice like "boil water" or "use a filter." That is incomplete. Biological contamination, chemical contamination, turbidity, storage contamination, and unsafe containers require different responses.

The tool should teach practical decision-making without pretending to replace laboratory testing or public-health guidance.

## MVP

- Water source risk questionnaire.
- Treatment decision tree.
- Boiling guidance.
- Settling and cloth pre-filtering guidance.
- Filter selection explanation.
- Chemical disinfection cautions.
- Safe storage checklist.
- Print-friendly one-page emergency sheet.

## Technical Plan

- Static PWA.
- No backend.
- Decision tree stored as JSON.
- Printable emergency cards.
- Accessibility-first mobile layout.

## Safety Rules

- Be explicit when water may be chemically contaminated.
- State that boiling does not remove many chemical pollutants.
- Warn that unclear or smelly water may need specialist treatment.
- Prefer conservative recommendations.
- Cite public-health sources before release.

## Portfolio Value

This project demonstrates responsible health-adjacent UX, offline-first design, decision-tree modelling, accessibility, and public-good product judgement.
